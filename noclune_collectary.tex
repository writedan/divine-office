	\vechapter{Introduction}
		\skiplines{-1}\vesectionp{Using this Book}
			This book contains all the collects for use in the hours of the liturgical year. The present volume contains only those parts, in the temporale, from the first Sunday in Advent through the octave of the Holy Innocents, and in the sanctorale from November 27 through January 4. 
			
			Where relevant, this book gives collects in two tones: the dominical and the ferial. Dominical tones are to be used on Sundays while ferial tones are used on days during the week when the collect of the Sunday is repeated. Ferial tones are also used for proper collects on certain weekdays. Saints' days, meanwhile, always use the dominical tone.
			
			Collects are usually preceded by this versicle:
			
			\gresetinitiallines{0}
			\skiplines{0.5}\gabcsnippet{(c3) <sp>v</sp> O(h) Lord,(h) hear(h) my(f) prayer.(g) <sp>r</sp>(::) And(h) let(h) my(h) cry(h) come(h) un(h)to(f) thee.(f) }
			
			The minister then always gives an invocation according to the solemnity of the liturgical office. There are three tones: a simple tone, used on ferial days and simple offices; a festive tone, used on semifestive and festive offices including most Sundays; and a solemn tone, used on solemnities. They are given in order:
			
			\skiplines{0.5}\gabcsnippet{(c3) Let(h) us(h) pray.(h) (::) Let(h) us(g) pray.(h) (::) Let(h) us(gh) pray.(h)}
			
		\vesection{Ceremonial}
			When praying collects, the minister always stands before the altar. He should face the altar while standing between it and the nave. He proceeds to this place before proclaiming any versicle, usually \enquote{O Lord, hear my prayer}, but the others also as they seldom occur.
			
			After giving the invocation \enquote{Let us pray}, he makes a brief genuflection. During the seasons of Septuagesima and Lent, as well as on vigil days, the congregation kneels during the proclaiming of the collect. On these days, the minister makes a profound genuflection, but still arises for the proclamation of the collect.
			
			All hours end with the same conclusion, except Prime and Compline which use a particular form described in the next sections. The other hours all have this form:
			
			\skiplines{0.5}\gregorioscore{common/conclusion.gabc}
			
			As with the proclamation of collects, the minister proceeds to stand before the altar before giving the versicle. Otherwise, unless he have another role to play, he remains in his place.

		\vesection{Anthems of Blessed Virgin Mary}
			Compline includes a final anthem of the Blessed Virgin Mary, which consists of the anthem itself, a versicle, the invocation \enquote{Let us pray}, and a collect. This immediately precedes the conclusion of Compline. Thus after the anthem chant is complete, the minister proceeds before the altar.
