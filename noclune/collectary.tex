	\vechapter{Brief Notes on Ceremony}
	
	If the minister is a priest, he should go before the altar, turn towards the people and give the salutation \enquote{The Lord be with you}, and then turning toward the altar say the versicle \enquote{O Lord hear my prayer}. But if he is a layman or deacon, he should only say the second versicle. After saying \enquote{Let us pray}, he should make a genuflection, then proclaim the prayer as appointed herein. After the people have responded \enquote{Amen}, he may return to his place, unless it is already the conclusion of the hour.
	
	This should also hold for the collect following the anthem of the Blessed Virgin Mary. Once the singing of the anthem is concluded, he proceeds before the altar, gives the appointed versicle, genuflects after \enquote{Let us pray}, and while still there concludes the hour.
